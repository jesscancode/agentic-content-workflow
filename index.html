The harness — shared services every article passes through
shared hub · single source of truth
Content Store
Article records + status, compliance corpus, brand & marketing KB, media library (DAM), link graph, author entities, KPIs. Every agent and script reads and writes here — nothing is copied by hand, so nothing drifts.
Custodian (agent) keeps it honest: dedup, schema, freshness, provenance
orchestration · not authority
Conductor
Routes each article through the pipeline, holds its status (editorial → compliance → brand → QA → live), fires triggers, enforces the gates, runs the audit cadences.
Moves the work — it never approves content. Humans decide at the gates.
the human gate

The agents — only where a script or a person could not do it better
Researcher — synthesises SERP + competitor gaps into prioritised titles (specialist-directed)
Writer — outlines, writes and optimizes for SEO + AI answers
Critic — adversarial quality + differentiation pass before human review
Compliance checker — RAG against the compliance corpus; flags unsupported claims with provenance
Brand checker — verifies CTAs, campaigns and product accuracy vs the brand KB
Analyst — scores performance vs KPIs, tags outcomes, proposes the next brief
Custodian — cross-cutting: keeps the Content Store clean and honest
Conductor — cross-cutting: routes work, holds status, enforces gates
Why not more. Media, semantic linking, JSON-LD, metadata, structural checks, publishing and CI/CD are rules-based and repeatable — they are automation, not agents (deterministic-first). Strategy and all five gates stay human. An agent is only added where the task genuinely needs judgment.

Scaling down. At low volume, one Reviewer agent can cover compliance + brand against both KBs, and the Writer can self-critique instead of a separate Critic. Split them out when volume or risk earns it — the same cold-start logic the framework uses.
Governance — the principles that keep it honest
  1. Deterministic-first — automate the repeatable, rules-based steps; reserve agents for judgment.
  2. Single source of truth — everything is generated from the Content Store, never copied by hand.
  3. Human gate on every decision — Gates 1–5; agents draft, a named owner approves.
  4. Provenance on every output — each claim traces to its source in the Store (compliance especially).
  5. One Responsible owner per stage — agent, script or person, never shared.
  6. Conductor is infrastructure, not authority — it routes and enforces, it never approves.
